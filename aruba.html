Question 2. Triangle traversal.

Here is a triangle of numbers. 
      1
     / \
    2   5
   / \ / \
  9   4  33
 / \ / \ / \
11  6  99   0
In each row (except for the last), the number is adjacent to two numbers on the row below it. For example, 9 is adjacent to 11 and 6. Imagine drawing a path connecting adjacent numbers from top to bottom. Take the sum of the numbers in this path. The lowest possible sum you could arrive at for this triangle is 13 (1 + 2 + 4 + 6).

2a. Write a program which will read a file describing a triangle such as this one, and determine the lowest possible sum of numbers which connect the top and the bottom. The input will be a tab-separated list of numbers, such as the following: 

1   
2       3   
30      50      4   
11      99      6       4   
3       17      9       332     12
(The shortest path in this triangle is 1 + 3 + 4 + 6 + 9).

You may assume the file will describe a well-formed triangle (with the correct number of integers per row). The sum of any path will not exceed 2 billion. There will never be more than 32,000 rows in a file, and there will generally be fewer. Your algorithm should not backtrack to previous rows (select only one number per row.)
